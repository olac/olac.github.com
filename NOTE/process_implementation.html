1. Site organization

The gateway for the Open Language Archives Community is hosted at http://www.language-archives.org/. The following are the pages in the root folder of the site:

/                    Home page
/organization.html   How OLAC is organized
/archives.html       Index of participating archives
/services.html       Index of participating services

The following are root-level folders:

/OLAC/    Holds documents in the standards track
/REC/     Holds documents in the recommendations track
/NOTE/    Holds docuemnts in the notes track
/src/     Holds XML source files for documents in the above three tracks
/docs/    Holds documents that are not official OLAC documents
/wg/      Holds the home pages for the working groups

An index page in each of these folders (except src) offers visitors a complete list of what is available.

2. Use of mailing lists

Mailing lists are used to implement much of the business of the community. The mailing lists are hosted at listserv.linguistlist.org.

Two lists are closed. Their subscriber lists are maintained by the OLAC Coordinators. In order to prevent subscription requests from the public at large, the lists are not made visible on the OLAC site nor on the mailing list archives page at Linguist List [LL-Archives]. The identity of the subscribers, however, is open and visible on the OLAC Organization page [OLAC-Organization]. The list archives can also be openly accessed via the form for accessing unlisted archives [LL-Unlisted]. The two closed lists are:

OLAC-Advisors

A mailing list that the OLAC Coordinators use for interacting with the OLAC Advisory Board, and which the board members may also use for interacting with each other.

OLAC-Archives

A mailing list that the OLAC Coordinators use for interacting with the Participating Archives and Services, and which the participants may also use for interacting with each other. There is only one subscriber per participating archive or service, and that is the person who is designated by the sponsoring institution to be the official contact person for OLAC business. At the discretion of the Coordinators, a contact person from selected Prospective Participants may also be subscribed.

All other lists are open. They are listed on the OLAC site and at Linguist List [LL-Archives]. They are set up in such a way that interested parties may subscribe themselves to the lists. There are two "built-in" public lists:

OLAC-General

A mailing list that the OLAC Coordinators use to make Calls for Pariticipation, Calls for Review, and other public announcements to the full list of Participating Individuals. This list is set up as a moderated list so that the only traffic on it is annoucements from OLAC.

OLAC-Implementors

A mailing list for a working group consisting of all those who are implementing the OLAC standards or are interested in doing so. While a working group is, in general, constituted for the purpose of developing designated documents and may dissolve once its documents are completed, this working group is created as a perpetual group to support those who are implementing the standards. It may also develop documents, but its existence is not dependent on this. This list is unmoderated so that implementors may interact freely with each other.

For every other working group, there is an open and unmoderated mailing list with a name of the form: OLAC-Groupname

3. Working group pages

Every working group has a home page on the OLAC site. That page includes the following information in a format which is uniform across all working group pages:

  • the name of the working group

  • a statement of purpose for the working group

  • the name and email address of the chair

  • links to the mailing list which offer the following functions: list group members, read mail archive, join group, resign from group

  • links to ballots that are currently open

  • links to results of ballots that have closed

  • a listing of the documents of the working group

The document listing should include both documents that are in progress and those that are. Only the latest version of each document should be listed since each version of a document gives a link to its previous version (and so on). For each document the following information should be shown:

  • the name of the document (as a link)

  • the type of the document

  • the status of the document

  • the latest date of issue

  • an optional comment describing the next major milestone and a target date
